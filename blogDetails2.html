Addressing Concerns About Chemical Peels

Published on: 07-08-2024 | Readtime: 5 min

In our practice, I see a lot of patients who get scared of getting a chemical peel done when suggested in the treatment plan. Gradually, on asking what scares them about the procedure, I found out that the general questions which are there in everyone’s minds. Today, I answer those worrisome questions.

1. Will our skin actually “PEEL” off after the procedure?

When we say chemical peel, it means chemo-exfoliation, yes, but will you be pulling off your skin from your face like the YouTube videos? No! There will be some amount of superficial peeling which you will face.

2. Different Peels Have Different Depths of Action

Depending on what your doctor is recommending the procedure for. In Indian skin tone, we usually would not go for anything that would be too deep.

3. How Many Indications Are There for a Peel?

There are a variety of peels available for use, consisting of Alpha hydroxy acids or Beta hydroxy acids or certain antioxidants as well. Sometimes there are combination peels consisting of these ingredients in fixed concentrations.

4. Specific Peels for Specific Conditions

If you have acne (pimples) or scars, your dermatologist might advise you peels with Salicylic Acid or retinols. If you have pigmentation issues, you might be advised a peel consisting of Glycolic acid, Kojic acid, Arginine, or Vitamin C, amongst many others.

5. Does Your Skin Start Thinning After Multiple Sittings?

No, your skin does not start thinning. Peels exfoliate your skin in a controlled manner and stimulate regeneration, giving you an even skin tone and the glow that you desire.

For acne or pigmentary conditions, we advise 3-5 sessions, and the effect can be maintained with certain creams or gels. As aging is a non-stop process, regular sessions are hence advised in such cases.

6. Any Side Effects?

If done by a qualified dermatologist, no side effects are faced. After any procedure, your dermatologist will advise you on strict sun protection, which applies to chemical peels as well.

7. How Long Does One Procedure Take?

It is an office procedure and usually will not take more than 15-30 minutes.

8. Contraindications

Any active infection (bacterial, viral, or fungal), keloidal tendency, facial dermatitis, photosensitizing drugs, pregnancy, and unrealistic expectations (any procedure takes time to give the desired results depending on your skin type and condition) are contraindications.
